Flowering plants are plants which
produce flowers as their reproductive
structures. They come in a great
variety of shapes and sizes and
include grasses, sedges, rushes,
many trees, as well as familiar plants
like daffodils and daisies. Flowers
often, though not always, have
brightly coloured petals. The flowers
conceal inside them the female
ovules which will be fertilised by
pollen grains. The pollen grains are
the male parts of the flower. Fertilised
ovules develop into the seeds from
which new plants will grow. The fact
that the ovules are protected inside
flowers has contributed greatly to the
success of flowering plants.
